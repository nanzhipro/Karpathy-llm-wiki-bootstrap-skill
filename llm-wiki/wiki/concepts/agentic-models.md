# Agentic Models

In this wiki's second source, "agentic models" refers to frontier systems that can work through extended technical tasks with some operational autonomy, especially when given tools such as a computer terminal [Karpathy x: AI Capability Perception Gap](../sources/karpathy-x.md). The source highlights [[openai-codex]] and [[claude-code]] as examples. `(high confidence)`

## Properties Emphasized By The Source

- They are strongest in technical work such as programming, math, and research.
- They can sustain longer task chains than lightweight chat interactions.
- Their capabilities become much more visible when they can act on a tool surface rather than only answer in plain chat.

## Why This Matters

The source uses agentic systems to explain why some technically sophisticated users perceive AI progress as much steeper than casual users do. That observation feeds directly into [[ai-capability-perception-gap]] and [[peaky-capabilities]].
