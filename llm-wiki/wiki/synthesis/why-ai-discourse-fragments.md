# Why AI Discourse Fragments

The source's central synthesis is that disagreement about AI capability is often less about ideology than about sampling bias across products, tiers, and domains [Karpathy x: AI Capability Perception Gap](../sources/karpathy-x.md). People who mainly encounter free or consumer-facing systems see obvious failures; people who use paid agentic coding systems in technical work see unusually steep capability gains. Both experiences are real, but they do not generalize equally well across the whole landscape. `(high confidence)`

## Fragmentation Mechanism

Three forces combine:

1. **Different products**: public-facing tools and frontier technical tools are not interchangeable
2. **Different domains**: capabilities are currently uneven, which is captured in [[peaky-capabilities]]
3. **Different incentives**: model builders appear to prioritize domains with stronger business value and clearer training signals

## Current Working Interpretation

This source suggests that public AI arguments should be segmented more carefully:

- by access tier
- by product surface
- by domain of competence
- by whether task success can be objectively verified

Without those distinctions, discussions about "AI capability" collapse too many different systems into one bucket.
