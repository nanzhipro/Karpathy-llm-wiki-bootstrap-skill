# AI Capability Perception Gap

The source argues that people hold sharply different beliefs about AI capability because they are sampling different products, tiers, and use cases [Karpathy x: AI Capability Perception Gap](../sources/karpathy-x.md). Someone judging from free or older consumer experiences may conclude that AI remains unreliable, while someone using frontier agentic coding systems may conclude that the capability curve is now extremely steep. `(high confidence)`

## Main Drivers Named By The Source

1. **Recency gap**: many opinions are anchored to older model generations
2. **Tier gap**: free or neglected product surfaces do not reflect the strongest paid systems
3. **Domain gap**: the biggest improvements are concentrated in technical domains rather than general advice or writing

## Consequence

Debates about AI often become confused because both sides are generalizing from real experiences, but those experiences come from different parts of the product and capability landscape. This is why the source says the groups are "speaking past each other."
